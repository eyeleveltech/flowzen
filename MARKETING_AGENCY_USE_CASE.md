# How to Use Flowzen for Your Marketing Agency

Flowzen is designed to streamline your agency's entire workflow—from closing a new client to managing their monthly social media calendars and delivering high-quality video creatives. 

This guide explains how your team (designers, video editors, and social media managers) can structure **Projects** and **Tasks** in Flowzen to keep everything organized.

---

## 1. Structuring Your Projects

In Flowzen, a **Project** should represent a major, ongoing engagement or a large one-off campaign for a specific client.

### Example Project Types:
- **"Monthly Social Media Management - [Client Name]"**: Use this for recurring monthly retainers where you design posts and manage their accounts.
- **"Summer Ad Campaign Video Production - [Client Name]"**: Use this for a specific, finite goal like a large video shoot and editing process.
- **"Brand Identity Redesign - [Client Name]"**: Use this for logo creation, poster designs, and brand guidelines.

**Best Practice:** Always link the Project to the specific Client in the CRM so you can see all revenue and activity tied to that client in one place.

---

## 2. Breaking Down the Work into Tasks

Once you have a Project, you break it down into actionable **Tasks**. A task is a specific deliverable assigned to one person, with a clear deadline.

### Scenario A: Creating Social Media Posters
For your graphic designers creating weekly content.

1. **Create the Task**: "Design 3 Instagram Posters for Week 1"
2. **Assign To**: Your Graphic Designer (e.g., Sarah).
3. **Due Date**: Thursday at 5 PM.
4. **Project Link**: Link it to "Monthly Social Media Management".
5. **Task Status Flow**:
   - `TODO`: The designer knows they need to work on it.
   - `IN_PROGRESS`: The designer is actively creating the posters in Illustrator/Photoshop.
   - `IN_REVIEW`: The posters are uploaded as attachments to the task, and the Account Manager is reviewing them.
   - `DONE`: Approved and ready to be posted.

### Scenario B: Video Editing Workflow
Video editing usually involves multiple hands and heavy files.

1. **Create the Task**: "Edit 30-Second Reel for Summer Sale"
2. **Assign To**: Your Video Editor (e.g., David).
3. **Description**: Include links to the raw footage on Google Drive/Dropbox and the script.
4. **Sub-Tasks / Comments**: Use task comments to handle revisions. If the client asks for a change, the Account Manager can comment on the task: *"Client requested we make the logo bigger at 0:25."*
5. **Task Status Flow**:
   - `TODO` $\rightarrow$ `IN_PROGRESS` $\rightarrow$ `IN_REVIEW` (Internal Review) $\rightarrow$ `DONE` (Exported & Delivered).

### Scenario C: Social Media Publishing
For your social media managers actually scheduling and posting the creatives.

1. **Create the Task**: "Schedule Week 1 Content across IG, FB, and LinkedIn"
2. **Assign To**: Social Media Manager.
3. **Priority**: Set to `HIGH` because missing a posting schedule hurts campaign performance.
4. **Dependencies**: This task cannot be marked `DONE` until the designer's task ("Design 3 Instagram Posters") is `DONE`.

---

## 3. Daily Workflow for Your Team

Here is how different roles in your agency use Flowzen every day:

- **The Account Manager / Agency Owner**: Looks at the **Dashboard** and **Pipeline** to see total revenue and new leads. They look at the **Projects** view to ensure no campaign is falling behind schedule.
- **The Designer & Video Editor**: Logs into Flowzen and immediately clicks on the **Tasks** page. They switch it to "List View" to see a simple, prioritized list of exactly what creatives they need to design or edit today.
- **The Social Media Manager**: Uses the Tasks page to track which creatives have moved to `DONE` status so they can grab the assets and schedule them in their posting tools.

## Summary

By using Flowzen:
1. **No more lost files**: Attach creative briefs directly to the tasks.
2. **Clear ownership**: Everyone knows exactly who is editing the video and who is posting it.
3. **Happy clients**: Projects are delivered on time because managers have a bird's-eye view of all overdue tasks.
